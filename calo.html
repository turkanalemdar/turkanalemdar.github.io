UX Methods & Skills

Competitive Analysis

Getting to know the key competitors in the market can provide a lot of ideas of what users might expect from the product and help me to determine which of your competitors are doing a good job at solving user problems and opportunities to meet an unfulfilled user needs. That's why I created a competitor profile for each of the competitors by focusing on their key objectives, overall strategy, and their market advantage. Also, I conducted SWOT Analysis to have a good feel for my competitors' products.

Apart from marketing analysis, I focused on UX Competitor Analysis and focused on the key points:

  • Usability
  • Layout
  • Navigation Structure
  • Compatibility
  • Differentiation
  • Calls to Action

User Stories

To to refine my requirements to small and executable parts, I created user stories that explain the specific actions a certain type of user can take within the application.

User Research

I conducted survey as well as user interviews to identify the users's general attitudes, and learn about their opinions on competing applications, and to collect their thoughts the features that they enjoy or leave them frustrated.

User Persona

To be able to establish empathy with the users and prioritize the functionality based on the audiences, I created user personas.

Information Architecture - Card Sorting

To understand how the users expect the content to be organized and decide what to put on the homepage, I conducted closed card-sorting.

In closed card-sorting, the participants are asked to group website content into predefined categories.

User Flow & Task Analysis

To be able to ensure that the users' needs have been satisfied at every point in the user flow, I concentrated on the user flows to make my designs more user-centered.

Wireframing & Prototyping

With the low fidelity wireframing, I planned the information hierarchy of a page, specifically how space should be allocated, where content will go, and what functionalities will be available. Moreover, I decided on the positioning of menu items, headers, and buttons.

To create an interactive experience for testing before a design is finalized, I created an interactive and clickable prototype using Figma. As a base for my design decisions, I often applied for the Material Design by Google.

Usability Testing

I conducted moderated test with scenario tasks and a SUPR-Q questionnaire. By performing usability testing, I evaluated the ability or inability of users to complete specific tasks and I rated the errors based on Jacob Nielsen's error scale.

I interpreted the information from the usability tests via an affinity mapping.

I processed the the information in the affinity mapping using the Rainbow Spreadsheet. I classified the important observations and errors and offered the possible solutions. I updated my prototype based on the errors I identified as being most critical.
